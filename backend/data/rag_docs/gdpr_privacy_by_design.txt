GDPR Privacy by Design and Default (Article 25)

Article 25 requires controllers to implement privacy by design and by default.
- Build technical and organizational controls into system architecture.
- Default settings must minimize collection, access, and exposure.
- Apply pseudonymization where feasible for risk reduction.
- Maintain evidence that safeguards were considered during design.

Recital 78 alignment:
- Encourage data minimization, processing limitation, and secure handling.
- Require measures supporting timely compliance verification.

Operational mapping:
- New model inputs or data fields should trigger privacy-by-design review.
