Article 5 GDPR states that personal data processing must be lawful, fair, transparent, purpose-limited, data-minimized, accurate, and protected with integrity and confidentiality.
Article 6 GDPR provides lawful bases for processing, including consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, and legitimate interests.
Article 17 GDPR grants right to erasure where personal data is no longer necessary or consent is withdrawn, except where retention is required by legal obligation.
Article 25 GDPR establishes privacy by design and by default for systems handling personal data.
Article 30 GDPR requires records of processing activities and controllers must maintain demonstrable accountability.
Articles 44 through 49 GDPR restrict international transfers and require safeguards such as SCCs or adequacy decisions.
