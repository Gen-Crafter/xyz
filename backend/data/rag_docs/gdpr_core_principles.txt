GDPR Core Principles and Lawful Basis (Curated Operational Notes)

Article 5 Principles:
- Process data lawfully, fairly, and transparently.
- Collect data for explicit and legitimate purposes only.
- Minimize data to what is necessary for the task.
- Keep data accurate and up to date.
- Apply storage limitation and defined retention windows.
- Preserve integrity and confidentiality using technical safeguards.
- Maintain accountability evidence for each processing activity.

Article 6 Lawful Basis:
- Consent for specific, informed, and revocable processing.
- Contract necessity when processing is needed to perform a contract.
- Legal obligation where law requires processing or retention.
- Vital interests for urgent life-or-death situations.
- Public task when authorized by law.
- Legitimate interests balanced against rights and freedoms.

Operational mapping:
- If no lawful basis exists, processing must be blocked.
- If lawful basis changes, re-registration and policy review are required.
