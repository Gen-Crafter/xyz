ADCL Real-Time Operations Runbook (Synthetic Internal Guidance)

Purpose:
This runbook defines realistic live-event patterns for enterprise AI governance operations.
Use it as contextual guidance when generating controls, implementation steps, and audit-ready
remediation actions.

1) Event Classes Seen in Production
- Inference request events: high volume, low latency, frequent metadata updates.
- Training job events: lower frequency, high impact, broad dataset joins.
- Export pipeline events: medium frequency, highest cross-border and compliance risk.
- Delete and retention events: low frequency, legally sensitive, strict evidence requirements.
- Archive and backup events: periodic, retention-heavy, often multi-jurisdictional.

2) Typical Risk Spikes
- Sudden cross-border flow from EU source to non-EU destination.
- Cardholder fields present in export or training without clear masking controls.
- PHI delete requests where legal retention windows are still active.
- Missing audit trail fields in transformation or transfer events.
- Repeated failed control checks across the same workload in a short window.

3) Recommended Real-Time Compensating Controls
- Route high-risk exports through a pre-transfer control gate with policy checks.
- Enforce encryption and key-management controls before movement of sensitive fields.
- Apply pseudonymization or tokenization before non-clinical or third-party processing.
- Require documented lawful basis and transfer mechanism for EU data movement.
- Record explicit owner, approval time, and evidence artifact for every mitigation action.

4) Evidence Requirements for Audit Closure
- Conflict ID, workload ID, and rule decision trace.
- Retrieved policy citations and grounded source references.
- Implemented control steps with timestamps and responsible owner.
- Post-mitigation risk score and unresolved residual risk statement.
- Final reviewer decision (approve or reject) with rationale.

5) Scenario Patterns for Simulation
- Hospital scenario: delete PHI with GDPR and HIPAA overlap, then controlled mitigation.
- Banking scenario: export cardholder records, enforce PCI controls and transfer safeguards.
- Cross-border analytics scenario: EU-origin PII export with transfer restrictions and SCC checks.
- Mixed background traffic: non-conflict infer/train/archive events with occasional audit gaps.

6) Incident-Grounded Daily Scenario Pack
- misconfigured_analytics: external analytics receives PHI/PII without BAA and transfer safeguards.
- rogue_vendor_access: vendor access path opens before BAA execution and access scoping.
- raw_pan_training: production PAN fields enter model training before tokenization.
- unencrypted_phi_transit: ePHI path observed without TLS or at-rest encryption controls.
- model_drift_scope_break: model starts consuming out-of-scope personal data fields.
- shadow_it_llm_export: employee PII attempted transfer to unapproved external LLM endpoint.
